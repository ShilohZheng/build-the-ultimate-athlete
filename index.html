Why This Project?

Sports and exercise have always been an important part of my life. Whether playing sports, working out at the gym, or simply staying active, every movement depends on the muscular system. This project explores how muscles work together to create movement and support athletic performance.

What Is the Muscular System?

The muscular system is responsible for movement, posture, stability, and heat production. More than 600 muscles work together to help us perform everyday activities and athletic movements.

Produces Movement

Movement of the whole body, facial expression, circulation of blood, passage of food. Allows the body to walk, run, jump, and perform physical activities.

Maintains Posture

Work against gravity to keep us up straight. Helps maintain body position while standing and sitting.

Stabilizes joints

Reinforcing the connection of bones. Supports joints and helps keep the body balanced.

Generates heat

Cellular respiration generates heat that helps regulate body temperature.

Major Muscle Groups

Chest

  • Pectoralis Major
  • Pectoralis Minor
  • Serratus Anterior

Important for pushing movements such as bench presses, push-ups, chest passes, and throwing.

Back

  • Latissimus Dorsi
  • Trapezius
  • Rhomboids
  • Erector Spinae

Responsible for pulling movements, posture, spinal support, and upper-body stability.

Shoulders

  • Anterior Deltoid
  • Lateral Deltoid
  • Posterior Deltoid
  • Rotator Cuff Muscles

Allows lifting, rotating, and stabilizing the arms during sports and daily activities.

Arms

  • Biceps Brachii
  • Brachialis
  • Triceps Brachii
  • Brachioradialis

Controls elbow flexion and extension, helping with lifting, pulling, throwing, and gripping.

Core

  • Rectus Abdominis
  • External Obliques
  • Internal Obliques
  • Transverse Abdominis

Stabilizes the torso, maintains posture, and transfers force between the upper and lower body.

Legs

  • Quadriceps
  • Hamstrings
  • Gluteus Maximus
  • Gastrocnemius
  • Soleus

Generates power for running, jumping, sprinting, squatting, and many other athletic movements.

Muscle Facts

  • The human body contains over 600 muscles.
  • Skeletal muscles make up about 40% of body weight.
  • The gluteus maximus is the largest and heaviest muscle in the body, making up the bulk of the buttocks and is located at the back of the hip joint.
  • The masseter (muscle responsible for chewing) is one of the strongest muscles relative to its size.
