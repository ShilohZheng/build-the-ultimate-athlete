Muscle Fiber Explorer

Compare the characteristics of fast-twitch and slow-twitch muscle fibers.

Type II Fast-Twitch Fibers

  • Generate high force
  • Contract quickly
  • Fatigue faster
  • Used in sprinting and jumping

Type I Slow-Twitch Fibers

  • Excellent endurance
  • Resist fatigue
  • Efficient oxygen use
  • Used in long-distance events

Muscle Contraction Types

Learn how muscles create movement through different contractions.

Concentric Contraction

The muscle shortens while producing force.

Example: lifting a dumbbell during a bicep curl.

Eccentric Contraction

The muscle lengthens while resisting force.

Example: lowering a dumbbell during a bicep curl.

Isometric Contraction

The muscle produces force without changing length.

Example: holding a plank.

Recovery & Muscle Adaptation

Muscles grow and adapt during recovery, not during exercise.

Sleep

Sleep helps repair damaged muscle fibers and supports growth.

Stretching

Stretching improves flexibility and reduces muscle stiffness.

Nutrition

Protein and carbohydrates help muscles recover and rebuild.

Muscle Fatigue Lab

Different activities cause muscle fatigue in different ways.

Fast-Twitch Fatigue

Sprinting relies heavily on fast-twitch fibers which produce high force but fatigue quickly.

Slow-Twitch Endurance

Endurance athletes depend on slow-twitch fibers that resist fatigue.

Recovery Required

Recovery allows muscles to restore energy and adapt to training stress.
